Centuries of Italian Heritage

Villa Paesano stands as a testament to Italian craftsmanship and the enduring appeal of countryside living. Originally built in the 18th century as a noble family's summer retreat, the villa has witnessed generations of Italian history unfold in this tranquil valley.

The property's name, "Paesano," meaning "countryman" or "fellow villager," reflects its deep connection to the local community and the timeless tradition of Italian hospitality.

Thoughtful Restoration

Marcello and Valeria have undertaken a meticulous restoration that honors the villa's architectural heritage while incorporating modern luxuries. Original stone walls, hand-hewn beams, and traditional terracotta tiles have been preserved and enhanced.

The surrounding olive groves, some trees over 300 years old, continue to produce the golden oil that has sustained this land for generations, connecting guests to an unbroken chain of Italian agricultural tradition.
